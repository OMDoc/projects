Theorem

A special case of associativity of addition (THM584). [x + y] + y = x + [y + y]. Without a lemma giving Sx + y = S[x + y], we can't do the induction on x, and induction on y fails too. The solution is to generalize this to associativity. In terms of unification (PR00), the setvar P should go to lambda z . [x + y] + z = x + [y + z]. So, this is an example of how to use unification to generalize the problem to be shown by induction. This was suggested by Michael Kohlhase, Nov 2000.
